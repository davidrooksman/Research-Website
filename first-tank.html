The First Tank

So many played a part in its long and complicated development that it is not possible to name any individual as the sole inventor of the tank.[29] However leading roles were played by Lt Walter Gordon Wilson R.N. who designed the gearbox and developed practical tracks and by William Tritton whose agricultural machinery company, William Foster & Co. in Lincoln, Lincolnshire, England built the prototypes. [12][30] On 22 July 1915, a commission was placed to design a machine that could cross a trench 4 ft wide.
